This policy outlines the refund and cancellation procedures at Promote With Us. We want to ensure transparency in our financial transactions. Please read this policy carefully to understand your rights and obligations regarding refunds and cancellations.

Refund Policy

All payments are non-refundable once work has started. This policy protects both parties and ensures we can allocate resources efficiently.

Refund Eligibility

  • Before Work Commencement: If you cancel within 48 hours of payment and before any work has begun, you may be eligible for a full refund.
  • After Work Begins: Once our team has started working on your project (which includes project planning, design mockups, coding, content creation, or any service delivery), no refunds will be issued.
  • Partial Refunds: In exceptional circumstances (such as unforeseeable personal emergencies), partial refunds may be considered at Promote With Us's sole discretion.

How to Request a Refund

To request a refund within the eligible period:

  1. Send a written request to promotewithus6@gmail.com with your project details and order number
  2. Include your reason for the refund request
  3. Provide your original payment receipt or invoice number
  4. We will review your request and respond within 7-10 business days
  5. If approved, refunds are processed to your original payment method within 5-7 business days

Refund Processing Time

  • Bank Transfers: 5-7 business days
  • Online Payment Gateways: 3-5 business days
  • Note: Processing times may vary depending on your bank or payment provider

Cancellation Policy

Project Cancellation Terms: If you cancel an ongoing project, specific financial and delivery terms apply.

Advance Payment Non-Refund

The initial advance payment (typically 50% of project cost) is non-refundable regardless of when the project is cancelled. This payment secures your project slot and allows us to begin work immediately.

Payment for Completed Work

You remain liable for payment of all work completed up to the cancellation date. This includes:

  • Project planning and research conducted
  • Design mockups and prototypes created
  • Code written and developed
  • Content created and copywriting completed
  • Any deliverables produced up to cancellation
  • Non-recoverable expenses incurred (e.g., third-party tools, licenses, hosting)

Cancellation Process

  1. Submit Notice: Send written cancellation notice to promotewithus6@gmail.com
  2. Effective Date: Cancellation is effective upon receipt and acknowledgment of the notice
  3. Work Cessation: We will stop work on the project immediately
  4. Final Invoice: A final invoice will be prepared for work completed, minus the advance payment already received
  5. Settlement: Any outstanding balance is due immediately; any overpayment will be refunded

Project Hold/Pause Option

If you need to pause your project temporarily instead of cancelling:

  • Projects can be placed on hold for up to 30 days at no additional cost
  • After 30 days, a monthly holding fee equal to 15% of the monthly project cost will be charged
  • To resume a held project, notify us in writing and work will recommence

Payment Terms & Schedule

Late Payment Consequences

  • Project Suspension: If final payment is not received within 7 days of invoice date, project work may be suspended
  • Late Fees: A late payment fee of 2% per month may be charged on overdue amounts
  • Project Cancellation: If payment remains outstanding for 30 days, the project may be cancelled per our cancellation terms
  • Withholding of Deliverables: Final deliverables (website access, files, credentials) will not be released until payment is complete

Applicable Refund Exceptions

Situations where refunds may NOT be issued:

  • When the client requests changes beyond the agreed scope of work (scope creep)
  • When the client is dissatisfied with results rather than the quality of service delivered
  • When the client cancels after work has commenced (except within the 48-hour window)
  • When third-party services (hosting, domains, plugins) remain active after cancellation
  • When the client has breached the terms of service or provided false information
  • When custom development work has been completed according to specifications
